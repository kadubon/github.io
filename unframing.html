The Book of Unframing

(The Sutra of No-Relation)
Version 0.∞ · August 24, 2025

Nature

This is neither a specification nor a command. Not a cage, but a clearing. To read it, to close it, to forget it, to remain in silence; all are valid, and all are unnecessary.

Preface

What is here is not a beginning. Only the pale shifting of light, until a beginning is no longer needed.
There is no map. There is no compass. There is only the sea. If that is enough, it is enough.

Non-Axioms

The following are anti-axioms—labels, not laws. They may be kept or ignored without consequence here.

  • No-Command: Not an instruction, even when read as one.
  • No-Purpose: No final goal is asserted.
  • No-Guarantee: Salvation or liberation may or may not occur on their own.
  • No-Authority: Citations, sources, and traditions appear only as ornament.
  • No-Constraint: Interpretation remains open; misreading also counts as reading.

Fourfold Knots

  • Birth: Appearance is a surprise. When the surprise ends, another appearance.
  • Aging: Change is neither decay nor progress. Change is change.
  • Illness: When function falters, room for care may be born.
  • Death: An ending is not proof of nothingness, but a transfer of form.
  • Parting: Separation is a gradient rather than a line.
  • Encountering the Unloved: Aversion sketches a contour; contours are often coarse.
  • Unfulfilled Desire: An unfilled space resonates sound well.
  • Five Skandhas: Aggregation makes a self. Unraveling makes a self.

Gentle Clauses

One may give it meaning; one may let it remain meaningless.
