Research Hub Overview

This site is the home page for K. Takahashi's research program on auditable autonomous intelligence, no-meta / observable-only governance, deterministic replay, long-running agents, scientific availability, and claim certification.

The site is organized so that readers can start with a broad map, follow focused series indexes, and then move to DOI-linked papers or related OSS.

Structured metadata, RSS, and citation files are provided as supporting navigation and indexing aids.

Core Research Areas

  • No-meta / observable-only governance: AI governance without privileged meta-judges, using observable evidence, audit logs, fail-closed verification, and public accountability.
  • Auditable autonomous intelligence: deterministic replay, claim certification, lifecycle audits, and bounded verification for long-running AI systems.
  • Long-running agents and memory governance: finite context, memory drift, recursive self-improvement, multi-agent operation, and workflow stability.
  • Constraint Generative Theory: a viewpoint-oriented theory for reading claims, reports, support conditions, scientific availability, and abstention.
  • Research automation and AI scientist protocols: audit-closed experimentation, reusable evidence, proof-carrying skills, and implementation-facing reference systems.
  • Formal foundations and persistence: persistent semantics, ontology drift, provenance, category-theoretic structure, and information-theoretic foundations.
